--- id: 693 title: "Will Blue Peter put their website in their 'capsule' now?!?!?!?" issue: 24 ---

Remember those old episodes of Blue Peter when they still had Valerie Singleton, John Noakes and Peter Purves on it?!?!??!?!?! They always kept doing this thing nearly every year where they'd get some kid from a local primary school to sift through their rubbish, and then stick it in a "time capsule" which was buried in the "Blue Peter Garden", (What happened to that?!?!?! Did they pave it over and turn it into an accounting department or something!??!?!?!?!) so that in 25 years, if Blue Peter was still going, the presenters would be able to dig it up, and find out what rubbish they used to throw out then!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!

Well, now someone at MIT have decided to do a webby version of it!!!!!!!!!! This "digital Time capsule" is going to be stored for 5 years- that may not sound like a lot of time, but in dog years, as well as internet years, it's actually quite a long time!!!!!!!!!!!! And like the "proper" time capsule, it's mostly filled with complete rubbish!!!!!!!!! Check this out: it's going to contain:

  • "An online guide that helps parents talk to their kids about the impeachment of President Clinton, along with an audio clip of his Senate impeachment trial, as played over a local radio station's Web site."
  • "A snapshot of Federal Reserve chairman Alan Greenspan's 28 January Senate testimony on the Net-stock mania, along with portions of Web pages and news accounts of the seemingly unrelenting wave of Internet deals.
  • "A page from online auction house eBay and cyberspace offerings from more traditional retailers such as Spiegel and Victoria's Secret. The latter hosted a much-ballyhooed webcast of a lingerie fashion show."
  • "Women-oriented sites such as Advancing Women, a service designed to help working women, and Women-Connect-Asia, an online network for women living and working in Asia."

Bet you can't wait to see the reaction when that capsule's re-opened, huh!!!!!!!!
